Abstract

We use the relation between Drinfeld-Sokolov Korteweg-de Vries (KdV) hierarchies and their super-extensions and conformal field theory (CFT) in the quantization procedure. The quantization of such models gives integrable structures for CFT, providing the possibility to study integrable perturbations. It appears that the quantization can be performed successfully in the case of models based on both (twisted) affine algebras and their super-extensions. In this talk, basic ideas of the quantization procedure are illustrated by two important examples: KdV hierarchies based on affine sl(2) algebra and affine osp(2|1) superalgebra.
